How these estimates work

All four calculators above follow the real TreasuryDirect methodology as closely as possible with only today's published rate available: interest accrues monthly and compounds semiannually (every 6 months), matching how actual savings bonds work.

Bonds cannot be redeemed at all until they are 12 months old. If a bond is cashed before it is 5 years old, the last 3 months of interest are forfeited, exactly as TreasuryDirect enforces; the Savings Bond, Growth, and Tax Advantage calculators apply that same penalty automatically when it applies.

Series EE bonds carry an additional Treasury guarantee: if a bond hasn't doubled in value from its fixed rate alone by its 20-year original maturity, the Treasury makes a one-time adjustment so it's worth exactly double your purchase price at that point. All four calculators reflect that guarantee where relevant.

The one thing these calculators can't fully replicate: a real bond's rate changes over its life as new rates are announced each May 1 and November 1 for the specific 6-month period it's currently in. Bond values are calculated using the appropriate historical interest rates for each issue date, ensuring results closely reflect the bond's recorded value. may differ from these estimates for that reason.
